The former school building today the artist-in-residency space GlogauAir.

In 2016, the Retune Conference will be transformed into the Retune Festival, an experimental laboratory for the development of new technologies and ideas. It’s all about inspiration, experience, and exchange. It will be a space for dialogue about the aesthetic-technological practices that are changing today’s society.

For seven days, an old school in Kreuzberg, Berlin will be transformed into a laboratory of the future. First, the Creative Production Lab will move into the space and bring small and middle-sized manufacturers from Eastern Germany together with entrepreneurs from the creative economy.

From the 6th to the 8th of October, the building opens up for all festival participants. These three days will be packed with experimental, frienzied, and intensive learning, thinking and making. There will be exhibitions, talks, and workshops grouped around four main themes. Presentations will generally be small and intimate, creating an environment for a direct exchange and collaboration. The presentations are scheduled to run in parallel, repeating at staggered times during the festival so that participants will be able catch everything.

The former classrooms will be turned into workshops, design laboratories, and offline chatrooms. International researchers, creatives, cyborgs, and technology-developers will transform the building into an enormous generator for inspiration. The festival is being curated by the Retune Team, and conceptualized and carried out by internationally renowned pioneers working on the border between art and technology.

The Retune Festival will also expand into the neighborhood surrounding the main venue, with satellite events, studio visits, and community meet-ups taking place in art spaces, cinemas, bars, and clubs.
